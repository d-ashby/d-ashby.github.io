learning plan 0.0

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

To become skilled enough to land a job as a software developer, it's too early to say specialties i want to focus on. Fundamentally I want to be in a position where I am able to learn more and be with other people who love to learn too.

A description of your strengths and limitations concerning learning

My strength is my drive to learn, my inquisitiveness, I suppose that is also my limitation as I realise I will need to keep focused on what really matters at the time. I also need to work on the core side of my skills not just technical!

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to build on my interpersonal skills and confidence with being out of my comfort zone socially. Be more social!

how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives?

I find it easier to learn when my body is kept healthy. Taking regular breaks, exercise, good sleep, and a healthy diet are my aims for my physical wellbeing (something I struggled with!). I hope that I will be able to keep my mental wellbeing healthy through being social and connecting with others, and be present for others who seek my council. If I keep these things in check I will be the best me for peers, facilitators and all others I will be interacting with during this bootcamp.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

I hope that by making a commitment and keeping to the steps I mentioned above, I won’t reach this point. But if I feel like I am losing my footing, I will have to reassess my position and seek advice from my peers and facilitators.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I will do my best to communicate any failings on my side. I will do what I can to contribute in the tenacity and success of the team.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

I expect the facilitators to guide and facilitate learning.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

I am lucky enough to give my full attention during this bootcamp.


Learning plan 0.1 (bootcamp edition)

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

To be a software developer - There are so many aspects that interest me within the world of programming. I hope that I will become skilled enough to help in developing programmes for people to use. At this stage what I have enjoyed developing the most so far is making games!

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My strengths and limitations still remain true. I have allowed myself the luxury of inquisition and dived down rabbit holes along the way. I find quite helpful but it does cost me the just in time learning approach.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

public speaking - I have done public speaking before as a teaching assistant at university. I dont think it ever gets easy and there is always room for improvement.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

My public speaking skills is something I would like to work on, sociability and relationship building is another aspect where I can grow.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

being positive and encouraging to eachother

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

To be open about progress and stuggles, to collaborate and be helpful when I can be. Much of what I wrote in my initial learning plan still remains true, I need to keep my physical and mental wellbeing in check so that I am in the best possible condition to learn. Building good habbits around exercise, nutrition and communication is the commitment I have made on myself so that I can be a positive component of a team of developers!
