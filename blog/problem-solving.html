Problem solving techniques

  • Pseudocode
    • - Pseudocode is verbalising the code. For example “ I want to add number a to number b and then post the result”. This is easier said than done! I feel like this is my key learning tool at the moment even if my pseudocode is wrong, it challenges my mind to think in the way that the code could work.
  • Trying something
    • o - I always try and tackle something first, even if it is garblegoop!
  • Rubber ducky method
    • - A rubber duck is great, trying to create sentences and hearing them puts that information in a new light and things may appear for what they truely are. I chat with a miniature viking that has a giant beard.
  • Reading error messages
    • - Often syntax is picked up and posted into the console, I always seem to forget a curly bracket, thanks error messages.
  • Console.logging
    • - This is useful for printing out smaller chunks of code. It’s a house of cards and sometimes checking things one by one by commenting things out and console logging lets me know whats going on. I find it helpful for understanding what the wild code that ive found on the web does.
  • Googling
    • - google has some sweet tools built in that help refine searches.Using key words is great applying operators to those keywords can yeild more accurate results, its strange that google doesnt have an easily accesible list of these operators. Here is a blog post explaining operators: Google Operators
  • Asking your peers for help
    • - Something I need to work on!
  • Asking coaches for help
    • - Something I need to improve.
  • Improving your process with reflection
    • - Dedicating a small amount of time to planning, and then time to process what has been completed has been helpful in creating , and new angles to approach my learning.
